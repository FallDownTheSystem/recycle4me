Waste guide

Here you will find information on how to sort your waste

Biowaste

    What to include into biowaste
  • fruit and vegetable peels
  • food waste
  • fish bones and bones
  • solidified grease
  • tea bags or leaves and coffee grounds, coffee filter paper
  • soft paper, such as kitchen towels, paper napkins, tissues
  • plant parts and dried flowers, small amounts of garden waste
  • wood-based litter from pet cages (for example, sawdust and pellets)

Carton

    What to include into carton waste containers
  • carton packages for liquids, such as milk and juice cartons (also with aluminium lining)
  • carton packages, such as cereal and cookie packs
  • paper packets, such as bread and flour bags
  • paper bags
  • egg cartons
  • shells of toilet paper and kitchen towel rolls
  • corrugated cardboard and kraft paper

Glass

    What to include into glass waste
  • drinking glasses
  • heat-resistant glasses (oven pans, coffee pans)
  • crystal glass
  • porcelain and ceramics
  • window glass
  • mirror glass
  • opal glass
  • lamps

Metal

    What to include into metal waste
  • Small metal items
  • food and beverage cans
  • ​aluminium foil and tins
  • metal lids and caps
  • metal casings of tea lights
  • empty and dry paint cans
  • empty aerosol cans
  • small metal items that can fit into the container
  • Scrap metal
  • bicycle
  • sheet metal and gutters
  • metal parts of furniture
  • metal pipes and cables
  • wood-burning sauna heaters
  • metal machines and devices
  • ​clean and open barrels

Paper

    What to include into paper waste
  • newspapers and magazines
  • adverts, brochures, postcards
  • envelopes
  • phone books and product catalogues
  • paperback books
  • hardcover books, if the covers are removed
  • copy paper and printed paper
  • sketch books and notepads
  • white paper bags

Plastic

    What to include into plastic waste
  • Empty plastic packages for food stuff
  • Empty plastic detergent, shampoo and soap bottles
  • Plastic carriers, bags and wrappings
  • Empty plastic bottles, canisters and jars, preferably flattened; caps and lids detached

Garden waste

    What to include into garden waste
  • leaves, grass and plant tops
  • chipped brushwood
  • rootstocks and tree stumps
  • apples from the ground

Mixed

    What to include into mixed waste
  • all plastics
  • disposable diapers, tampons and sanitary towels
  • worn out textiles and shoes
  • leather, faux leather and rubber
  • drinking glasses and heat tolerant glass dishes
  • porcelain and ceramics
  • vacuum cleaner bags
  • incandescent light bulbs and halogen light bulbs and fuses
  • ash and cigarette stumps
